Bunny Chow

Cultural Description

Bunny Chow is an iconic South African fast food that originated in Durban's Indian community during the 1940s. Despite its name, there's no rabbit involvedâ€”the dish consists of a hollowed-out loaf of bread filled with curry.

Created as a convenient way for Indian workers to carry their lunch, Bunny Chow has become a beloved street food that represents South Africa's diverse cultural heritage and the fusion of Indian and African culinary traditions.

Ingredients

For the Curry:

  • 2 lbs chicken, lamb, or beef, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon coriander
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 potatoes, cubed
  • 1 cup water or stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

For the Bread:

  • 1 unsliced loaf of white bread (or 4 individual bread rolls)

Cooking Instructions

Click to expand step-by-step instructions
  1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.
  2. Add meat and brown on all sides, about 5-7 minutes. Remove and set aside.
  3. In the same pot, add onions and cook until golden, about 5 minutes.
  4. Add garlic and ginger, cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Stir in curry powder, turmeric, cumin, and coriander. Cook for 2 minutes.
  6. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  7. Return the meat to the pot, add potatoes and water/stock.
  8. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer covered for 45 minutes until meat is tender.
  9. If curry is too thin, simmer uncovered to thicken. Season with salt and pepper.
  10. While curry is cooking, prepare the bread: Cut the loaf into quarters (or use individual rolls).
  11. Hollow out the center of each bread piece, creating a bread bowl. Save the scooped bread.
  12. Fill each bread bowl generously with hot curry.
  13. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with the scooped bread on the side for dipping.

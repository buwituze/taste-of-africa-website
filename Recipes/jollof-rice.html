Jollof Rice

Cultural Description

Jollof Rice is one of West Africa's most iconic dishes, sparking friendly rivalries between countries like Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal over who makes it best. This vibrant one-pot meal combines rice with a rich tomato sauce, seasoned with a blend of aromatic spices and often served at celebrations, gatherings, and special occasions.

The dish represents the warmth and communal spirit of West African culture, bringing families and friends together around a shared meal.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons tomato paste
  • 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (optional, for heat)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, bell peppers)

Cooking Instructions

Click to expand step-by-step instructions
  1. Heat the vegetable oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the diced onion and saut√© until translucent, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
  3. Stir in the tomato paste and cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring constantly to prevent burning.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes, curry powder, thyme, and bay leaves. Stir well and let simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Add the chicken broth and bring to a boil. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Add the rice and scotch bonnet peppers (if using), stir to combine, and reduce heat to low.
  7. Cover the pot tightly and cook for 25-30 minutes without lifting the lid.
  8. After 25 minutes, check if the rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. Add mixed vegetables on top.
  9. Cover again and cook for 5 more minutes.
  10. Remove from heat, fluff with a fork, and serve hot.

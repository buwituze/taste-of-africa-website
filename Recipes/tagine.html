Moroccan Tagine

Cultural Description

Named after the distinctive cone-shaped earthenware pot in which it's cooked, tagine is a cornerstone of Moroccan cuisine. This slow-cooked stew combines savory and sweet flavors, reflecting the rich culinary heritage of North Africa influenced by Berber, Arab, and Mediterranean traditions.

The tagine pot's unique design allows steam to circulate during cooking, creating tender, flavorful dishes that are often served at family gatherings and special celebrations.

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs lamb or chicken, cut into pieces
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron threads
  • 2 cups chicken broth
  • 1 cup dried apricots
  • 1/2 cup almonds, toasted
  • 2 carrots, sliced
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • Fresh cilantro and parsley
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Cooking Instructions

Click to expand step-by-step instructions
  1. Heat olive oil in a tagine or heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
  2. Season the meat with salt and pepper, then brown on all sides for about 5-7 minutes.
  3. Add the chopped onion and garlic, cooking until softened.
  4. Stir in all the spices (cumin, coriander, cinnamon, ginger, saffron) and cook for 1 minute until fragrant.
  5. Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a boil.
  6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 45 minutes.
  7. Add carrots, zucchini, and dried apricots. Cover and cook for another 20-25 minutes.
  8. Check that the meat is tender and vegetables are cooked through.
  9. Garnish with toasted almonds and fresh herbs before serving.
  10. Serve with couscous or flatbread.
